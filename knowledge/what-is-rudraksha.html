The Origin

What is
Rudraksha?

The tears of Lord Shiva, a divine gift to humanity.

शिव के अश्रु — मानवता के लिए एक दिव्य उपहार

The Mythological Origin

According to the Shiva Purana, Lord Shiva spent thousands of years in deep meditation for the well-being of all living creatures. When he finally opened his eyes, a few drops of tears fell to the earth entirely out of compassion. Wherever these tears landed, the Rudraksha tree (*Elaeocarpus ganitrus*) was born.

The word "Rudraksha" itself is formed from two Sanskrit words: Rudra (a name for Lord Shiva) and Aksha (eyes or tears). Thus, it physically translates to "The Tears of Shiva."

The Scientific Angle

Scientifically, Rudraksha is the dried seed of the fruit from the Elaeocarpus ganitrus tree. These trees grow in specific high-altitude regions, predominantly in the Himalayas, Nepal, and certain parts of Indonesia.

Authentic Rudraksha beads are known for their unique electromagnetic properties. When worn against the skin, they act as a stabilizing anchor, helping to regulate the heart rate, reducing stress, and bringing the body's biorhythms into a unified state of calm.

The Mukhi (Faces)

Every Rudraksha bead has natural clefts or lines running from its top (Mukh) to its bottom. These lines are called "Mukhis." Based on the number of these lines, Rudraksha are classified from 1 Mukhi up to 21 Mukhi.
