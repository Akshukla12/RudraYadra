The Discipline

How to Wear
Rudraksha

Maintaining purity and maximizing spiritual benefit.

The Correct Metal & Thread

Your Rudraksha should ideally be worn in a metal that conducts energy well. Silver and Copper are the most recommended metals due to their cooling and grounding properties. High-quality Gold can be used for fiery beads depending on your astrological chart. Alternatively, weaving it in a strong, clean red or yellow silk thread is perfectly aligned with Vedic practices.

The Activation Ritual (Prana Pratishtha)

A bead that hasn't been energized is simply jewelry. At Rudra Yatra, we energize every bead before it reaches you. However, to synchronize it specifically to your personal frequency:

  • Wash the bead gently with raw milk and pure water on a Monday morning.
  • Apply a small dot of sandalwood (chandan) paste to the bead.
  • Hold it in your right hand and chant the seed mantra of your specific Mukhi, or simply "Om Namah Shivaya" 108 times.
  • Wear it with pure intent.

Daily Rules to Honor

The energy field established by a Rudraksha is delicate and must be respected.

  • DO NOT wear the bead while consuming non-vegetarian food or alcohol. Remove it, store it safely, and wear it back after bathing.
  • DO NOT wear it during funerals or times of extreme impurity.
  • DO NOT let others handle, wear, or touch your personal bead. It is uniquely tied to your energetic signature.

Myths vs. Facts

Myth: "Women shouldn't wear Rudraksha."

Fact: Shiva Purana explicitly states that anyone, regardless of gender, caste, or age can wear Rudraksha. Women are just advised to remove it during their menstrual cycle out of respect for energy fluctuations.
Myth: "You must give up all wealth to wear it."

Fact: Only a specific bead (like the 1 Mukhi) steers one heavily towards detachment without guidance. Beads like the 7 and 13 Mukhi are exclusively for generating material wealth and comforts.
